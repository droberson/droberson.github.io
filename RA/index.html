What Uses Radio?

  • WiFi, Bluetooth, Wireless Input Devices
  • NFC, RFID, EMV
  • Cordless phones (including cellular), pagers
  • Broadcast radio and television
  • Baby monitors, children's toys, drones
  • 2-way radios (resorts, police/fire, the feds!, military, transportation, construction, aviation, ...)
  • Garage and gate openers
  • Satellites (GPS, Iridium, Amateur, ISS, ...)
  • RADAR
  • Cars!
  • Lots and lots more...

Why Get Licensed?

  • Ultimate hacker hobby: hardware, software, skills carry directly over into IT careers.
  • Nearly limitless research possibilties.
  • Gain privileges to amateur bands
  • Social interaction and public service.
  • Access to a lot of interesting networks and systems: Echolink, DMR, Winlink, IRLP, repeater networks, ...
  • Obtain legal hands-on experience.
  • Great excuse if you get pulled over with tons of electronics.

How to Get Licensed

  • http://www.arrl.org/find-an-amateur-radio-license-exam-session
  • Study: ARRL Android/iOS app (works offline)
  • ARRL books. Check Amazon for used books.
  • Got mine for ~$5 each.
  • What to expect on the exam? (Seniors and "preppers")
  • How long until you get a call sign?
  • http://wireless2.fcc.gov/UlsApp/UlsSearch/searchLicense.jsp

Please Get Licensed!

  • Would like to talk to other people about stuff besides groceries, the weather, doctor's appointments, and their medical problems.
  • Plenty of clubs are in desparate need of IT savvy people and younger membership.
  • Help keep this hobby/art alive! Use it or lose it.
  • Lots of terrible software to audit or patch.
  • I've learned a LOT.

Scanning

  • Does not require a license.
  • Some traffic is illegal to intercept.
  • A lot of interesting traffic!
  • Scanners can be obtained for under $100
  • Can use an SDR and extra software to scan (~$30)
  • I prefer Uniden scanners and ProScan software for stationary setups. SDR and GQRX for temporary.
  • Python! https://github.com/suidroot/pybct15x
  • RadioReference.com provides a database of known frequencies.

Scanning Remotely

  • Determine frequencies to scan.
  • Raspberry Pi, GQRX, cheap SDR, network connectivity (under $100 if you have access to power + network on premesis)
  • Will need portable power + means for remote control (WiFi, 3G, extra $50+)
  • http://gqrx.dk/doc/remote-control
  • http://gqrx.dk/doc/streaming-audio-over-udp
  • Can transmit with HackRF + extra software tooling (most SDRs are receive only!)

Data Exfiltration w/ Radio

  • Cheap radios (Baofeng UV-5R) + open source software make an excellent out of band communications channel!
  • Minimodem Demo video
  • Requires sound cards. Cheap USB sound cards work great for this. http://www.outletpc.com/c5801.html
  • No network traffic! Does not trip IDS.
  • Slow. Wouldn't be suitable for dumping a database or file server. Hashes or small files would be great.
  • Can be used for remote control (SSH). Illegal because of encryption.

SDR

  • Can get started for very cheap.
  • Get one with SMA connector versus MCX if possible.
  • Most DO NOT TRANSMIT.
  • Abundance of information online.
  • GQRX (Mac, Linux)
  • SDR# (Windows)
  • GNURadio Companion https://wiki.gnuradio.org/
  • Tons of smaller tools depending on what type of data you are attempting to receive!

Fox Hunting

  • Methods for finding locations of rogue radio sites and stolen equipment.
  • Is a "sport" in amateur radio.
  • Directional antenna + spectrum analyzer.
  • Laptop + SDR + antenna.
  • RF Explorer + VAS Crosshair (~$420)

Baofeng Radios

  • Cheap, handheld Chinese radios.
  • ~$25-80 depending on model.
  • Great entry level radios!
  • TONS of material online.
  • A lot of older hams hate them (elitism).
  • Can be programmed manually, but I do not recommend it.
  • Program with CHIRP
  • Get the more expensive programming cables unless you're using Linux. Nothing but problems on Mac and Windows otherwise!

Repeaters

  • "Extend" range/power of radios.
  • One of the first things a new ham typically does.
  • PL tones.
  • Offsets.
  • "Linked" repeaters.
  • Build your own with two Baofeng radios.

DMR

  • Digital Mobile Radio
  • Requires compatible radio.
  • TYT MD-380 are best bang for the buck currently.
  • Supports text messaging and private calling.
  • Travis Goodspeed hacked firmware
  • A bit of a learning curve to program the radio.
  • CHIRP plugin offers limited support.
  • Ended up downloading a Windows VM and using the official software in the end.

War Driving

  • Travelling around cities with GPS and WiFi.
  • Kismet, giskismet, Google Earth.
  • Tried with WiFi Pineapple. Poor results.
  • Currently use ASUS Chromebook Flip, Kali Linux via Crouton, Alfa WiFi card, and Holux GPS. Works exceptionally well.
  • Other types of radio can be war driven: ZigBee

Conclusion

  • This barely scratched the surface.
  • A lot of interesting work still.
  • Audit/fuzz software. Radio software is often full of bugs. Not much scrutiny!
  • Some hardware can not or will not be patched.
  • Many protocols will never be fixed.
  • Lots of new gadgets use radio in one way or another. Be evil.
